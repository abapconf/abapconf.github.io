Mannheim: Exclusive Highlights

Participants in Mannheim have the additional option to participate in onsite exclusive sessions. These sessions are offered by SAP and our sponsors.

Demo Pods

Live demos and hands-on system access. Right from the labs: get your hands dirty and explore the latest features.

Expert Talks

Experts share the idea behind the product. Learn directly from the minds behind a product, get insights and learn get to ask your questions.

Influence Sessions

Experts from SAP are eager to get your feedback. Share what you are missing or what could be improved, directly with the people responsible at SAP.

Meet the Experts

Get to know experts from the SAP universe. The experts are onsite in Mannheim: from SAP, customers and partners. Exchange your ideas and make connections that last.

Additional onsite information

Expo

Make sure to check out the Expo. SAP and our sponsors are sharing some great technology and solutions you can try out. Use the chance to get in touch with real experts that are not there to sell you something, but to help solve a problem you have.

Wardrobe and luggage storage and departure

You can store your clothes and luggage at the event location at the Foyer. As this area is also accessible to other people working at Mafinex, we assume no liability for wardrobe and luggage storage. You should not have a problem finding a taxi or going to the train station.

WiFi

Free WiFi will be available. Credentials will be shared on-site. To ensure a good experience for everyone, do not use the WiFi for downloading files, watching movies or assisting the recording of previous ABAPConf conferences.

Evening event

After the conference, we plan to find a nice restaurant and hope that some of you can join us.

Explore Mannheim

ABAPConf 2026 takes place in Mannheim. In case you are arriving earlier or want to stay longer: congratulations, you are in one of the best regions in Europe.

For those staying a little bit longer, here are some tips to get to know the region.

Mannheim with its Quadrate and palace and is the city where Schiller and Mozart lived and worked. It’s also the city where the bicycle and car was invented. From there the Bertha Benz Memorial route starts and you can visit the world first gas station in Wiesloch.

Nearby you can visit Heidelberg, with its world famous university (founded in 1386), its Palace, the little Heidelberg Tun, the (really) large Great Heidelberg Tun, Philosophenweg, and the old town.

Schwetzingen offers a beautiful palace, is home of the Welde brewery and just like Mannheim, Mozart worked here.

Kraichgau, the land of the 1.000 hills. Perfect for everyone that likes outdoor activites: swimming, bike, running. From spring to autumn, you can find many sport events: Karlsruhe Baden Marathon, Heidelberg Half Marathon, Ironman 70.3 Kraichgau, Hockenheim Ring.

Going south is Karlsruhe with its - of course: palace - and its unique city layout. And actually the city where the bicycle and car were invented, and the periodic table, and the first mobile browser. Germanys first e-mail was also received in Karlsruhe.

For everyone interested in technologoy: there is the Technik Musuem Sinsheim and one in Speyer. The Speyrer Cathedral (1030) is the largest remaining Romanesque church (and if you have enough time: the Camino de Santiago passes through).

In case you like wine: Kraichgau, Baden and Palatinate are the wine regions in Germany. If you ever wanted to taste some of the best wines in the world: now is the time.
